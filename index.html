Everyone has potential


We do not believe in demonising and segregating addicts into lives of poverty, disease and crime. Using punishment as a deterrent doesn't work. The ruined lives and destroyed communities prove this. Everyone has the potential for a valuable life and valuable contribution. Addicts should be able to reconnect and contribute. The problem with addiction is not the addicts, but how they are treated. This sector of society have been forced to view the world in a particularly challenging way and if given a genuine way out, will be their powerful advantage.

"The opposite of addiction is not sobriety. The opposite of addiction is connection."

-Johann Hari

Introducing Project Phoenix
Our primary objective is to reduce the number of addicts unable to contribute to society. Our approach is to utalise blockchain and token economics to create a new socio-economic system around reconnecting addicts with their communities.

Psychology

Reconnection must be initiated by incentivising the change of negative mindsets towards addicts within communities.

Tokenomics

The incentive to actively support addicts can come from creating a financial reward for individuals supporting Project Phoenix.

Blockchain

Allows us the best opportunity to create a new socio-economic system around reintegrating addicts with society.

Expert Guided


The recovery, education and resettlement program will be designed by doctors, experts and supporters who have ideas they want to contribute. By combining a collaboration platform with a new voting mechanism, we can ensure that the program is responsive to new knowledge.

Adaptable to Communities


Each community will have different needs and requirements. Therefore each community should have a certain level of autonomy to adjust the program to best serve their addicts.

Benefit Communities


Once addicts return to working society, they pay back a percentage of their earnings - like a student loan. This profit will not be hoarded by a centralised corporation. Individuals in communities who supported addicts will be rewarded.
