The past doesn't equal the future.

Reconnecting addicts with society. Changing lives. Transforming communities.

Contribute

Potential is what could be


We do not believe in demonising and segregating addicts into lives of poverty, disease and crime. The ruined lives and ruined communities prove this doesn't work. Everyone has the potential for a valuable life and valuable contribution. Addicts should be able to reconnect and contribute. The problem is not the addicts, but how they are treated. Addicts have been forced to view the world in a particularly challenging way. That will be their powerful advantage.

"The opposite of addiction is not sobriety. The opposite of addiction is connection."

-Johann Hari

How To Make Change

Psychology

Recovery is merely one step in the process, reconnection must be initiated by altering negative mindsets of communities.

Economics

Economics can create an incentive to encourage and maintain support from individuals in communities.

Technology

New technology can be developed in a transparent, open source and collaborative manner. Ensuring that it best serves addicts and communities.


Only a solution that creates a link between addicts and society will be successful. We create and maintain this link through economics - by creating a financial reward for supporting Project Phoenix. This will initiate a psychological shift in communities from oppressing addicts, to actively wanting them to succeed. So that everyone benefits from addicts rediscovering a life worth living.

Community Owned


Addiction is a problem that affects every community in the world. One way or another, everyone is affected by it. Therefore, everyone should be able to benefit from the solution. Project Phoenix is owned by everyday people who help initially fund the project. The profits that are made when addicts re-enter working society are then distributed to these people. This creates an economic incentive for initial and coninued support.

Expert Guided


By bringing together worldclass Doctors and experts we can create a recovery, education and resettlement program that will give addicts the best possible chance of re-entering society.

Economic Concept

As addicts re-enter working society, they then pay back a small percentage of their wage to Project Phoenix - just like a student loan.

It is critical to the success of Project Phoenix that everyday people can easily become co-owners of the organisation. This co-ownership will increase the possibilities for initially funding Project Phoenix and will aid in the easy distribution of profits back to the communities.
